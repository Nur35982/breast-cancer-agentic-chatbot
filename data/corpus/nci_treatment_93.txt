Submuscular insertion of an artificial implant (silicone or saline filled).If an immediate implant cannot technically be performed, a tissue expander can be inserted beneath the pectoral muscle.
Saline is injected into the expander to stretch the tissues for a period of weeks or months until the desired volume is obtained.
The tissue expander is then replaced by a permanent implant.
For more information on breast implants, see theU.S.
Food and Drug Administration.
