The following protective factors and interventions reduce the risk of female breast cancer: Estrogen use (after hysterectomy).[32-34] Exercise.[35-37] Early pregnancy.[38-40] Breastfeeding.[41] Selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs).[42] Aromatase inhibitors or inactivators.[43,44] Risk-reducing mastectomy.[45] Risk-reducing oophorectomy or ovarian ablation.[46-49] For more information about factors that decrease the risk of breast cancer, seeBreast Cancer Prevention.
Clinical trials have established that screening asymptomatic women using mammography, with or without clinical breast examination, decreases breast cancer mortality.
